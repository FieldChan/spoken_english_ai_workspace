# Michael's Workplace English Phrasebook - Batch 1

## 1. Follow Up Progress

Chinese intent: Politely ask whether there is any update without sounding pushy.

Natural:

> Just wanted to follow up on this. Have you had a chance to take a look?

More conversational:

> Just checking in. Any updates on this?

More formal:

> I just wanted to check if there have been any updates on this.

Template:

> Just wanted to follow up on {topic}. Have you had a chance to {action}?

## 2. Ask About Blockers

Chinese intent: Ask whether the other side has run into any issues.

Natural:

> Let me know if there are any questions or blockers from your side.

More conversational:

> Any blockers on your side?

More formal:

> Please let me know if there are any concerns or blockers that we can help with.

Template:

> Let me know if there are any {questions / blockers / concerns}.

## 3. Clarify a Misunderstanding

Chinese intent: Clarify something politely.

Natural:

> Just to clarify...

More conversational:

> Just to clarify, this is actually...

More formal:

> I'd just like to clarify that...

Template:

> Just to clarify, this is not A, but B.

## 4. Request Support

Chinese intent: Ask someone for help.

Natural:

> Would you be able to help review the code when you have a chance?

More conversational:

> Could you help take a look?

More formal:

> Would you be able to help with...

Template:

> Would you be able to help {action}?

## 5. Thank Someone for Clarifying

Chinese intent: Respond after someone explains something.

Natural:

> Got it, thanks for clarifying.

More conversational:

> Ah got it, thanks for clearing that up.

More formal:

> Thanks for the clarification.

Template:

> Got it, thanks for clarifying.

## 6. Politely Move Something Forward

Chinese intent: Express hope that things can move forward soon without pushing too hard.

Natural:

> Hopefully we can move this forward soon.

More conversational:

> Hope we can get it moving soon.

More formal:

> It would be great if we could move this forward soon.

Template:

> Hopefully we can {action} soon.

## 7. Ask About Next Steps

Chinese intent: Ask what the next step should be.

Natural:

> Do I need to create a support issue for this?

More conversational:

> What's the next step from here?

More formal:

> Could you advise on the next steps?

Template:

> Do I need to {action}, or should {someone}?

## 8. Say Something Has Been Completed

Chinese intent: Tell others something has already been done.

Natural:

> We've already completed the development in London.

More conversational:

> We've already finished this on our side.

More formal:

> The development has already been completed.

Template:

> We've already {completed / finished / integrated}...

## 9. Make a Proposal

Chinese intent: Suggest an approach.

Natural:

> We have a proposal that might help.

More conversational:

> Maybe we can do it like this.

More formal:

> We would like to propose the following approach.

Template:

> We have a proposal that might help...

## 10. Express Alignment

Chinese intent: Say that you agree or are aligned.

Natural:

> Yes, that makes sense to me.

More conversational:

> Yes, we're on the same page.

More formal:

> Yes, I'm aligned with that approach.

Template:

> Yes, I'm aligned with {approach / plan / direction}.

## High-Priority Chunks

Practice these until they become automatic:

- Just wanted to follow up on...
- Have you had a chance to...?
- Let me know if there are any...
- Would you be able to help...?
- Could you advise on the next steps?
- Just to clarify...

## Common Chinglish Risks

Avoid:

> I want to explain...

Use:

> Just to clarify...

Avoid:

> I want to ask...

Use:

> Just checking...

Avoid overusing:

> Can you...?

Use when you want to sound more polite:

> Would you be able to...?
