## Self-Correction

Chinglish / weak version:

> You have posted a PR yesterday, I need your help to review it.

Better version:

> I posted a PR yesterday and would appreciate your help reviewing it.

Why it is better:

- The speaker posted the PR, so use "I posted", not "you have posted".
- "Would appreciate your help reviewing it" is more natural and polite than "I need your help to review it".
- Use "blockers" or "a blocker", not "any blocker".
- Split long follow-up messages into short sentences for clearer workplace chat.
